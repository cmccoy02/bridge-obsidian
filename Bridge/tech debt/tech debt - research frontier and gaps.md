# Tech Debt - Research Frontier and Gaps

Parent: [[tech debt]]

## Frontier Observations (as of 2026-02-24)
- AI coding tools shift debt formation rate, not debt physics.
- Empirical results on maintainability are mixed across controlled vs. production contexts.
- Verification capacity appears to be a systemic bottleneck in AI-accelerated teams.

## Open Research Gaps
- Standard metric for "verification debt" across organizations.
- Causal linkage between AI-generated change volume and long-term incident rates.
- Reliable estimation methods for debt interest in socio-technical systems.
- Unified taxonomy that integrates architecture, documentation, and AI-eval debt.
- Longitudinal studies on debt retirement ROI in AI-first teams.

## Measurement Gaps
- Most orgs track code smells but not architecture/doc debt quantitatively.
- Few teams connect debt records to customer impact and margin effects.
- Debt datasets are fragmented; little cross-company benchmarking.

## What Could Become Standard
- Debt accounting embedded in delivery telemetry.
- Evidence-backed debt grading (not intuition-only).
- AI-aware quality gates with reproducible eval baselines.
- Explicit cost-of-delay and cost-of-debt tradeoff models in planning.

## Bridge Thesis
The market needs a debt-intelligence layer that converts engineering telemetry into financial-grade debt decisions.
